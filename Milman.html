JOELLE'S MOOD RING

towards a vibes-based metrical analysis of Tel Aviv coffee shops

#ceasefirenow #ceasefireforever #ceasefireyesterday

Hello, Dear readers.

Joelle Milman here. I’m honored to be invited as a guest blogger for this fine platform. As a resident of Tel Aviv for almost four consecutive years (!!!wtf? too long), I’ve gained nothing if not a finer understanding of coffee – the drink, the culture, the shop. Ah, yes, there is nothing like a coffee shop. Tel Aviv is full of smog and bombs and issues, but the coffee shop culture is truly like nowhere else in the world. Where else can you sit for 12 hours, take off your shoes, make 12 new friends, and drink happily affected single-origin coffees and blends?

Thus in mind, I make this small contribution to the canon of the coffee shop by sharing some thoughts and rankings, here.

A healthy coffee shop culture in a city is evident when there are enough open & available shops that you are able to pick that suits your mood, and know that whatever space you choose will also support your functional needs. The latter metric, of functionality, is where Gil’s rankings shine: space, food, and sockets are pillars of functionality that make a shop viable for an extended sit. In this addendum, I aim to fill out that understanding with the elements of a coffee shop that truly make it what it is: call it the vibe. The proper coffee shop must be of the correct vibe to suit your wider mood. You won’t visit the space-age coffee shop with hard white benches and blank white walls (HOC florentin) when you’re trying to hang out with friends and take your shoes off (go to Dede for that). Sometimes you need to sit around a bunch of men with an espresso fetish and masculinity crisis (Waycup, haavoda) and sometimes you need children playing in the dirt and locals selling homemade soap (Cafe Shapira).

The factors, of course, are many: the chosen factors below were selected for they each include a wide array of factors. For example, Design of the space includes a full-scale analysis, from lighting to decor to seating options. “People” references all humans who populate the space, from patrons to baristas. These coffee shop reviews will also focus on the thing that Gil somehow forgets - the coffee.

So in summation. This review aims to add some new cafes to the list and, in doing so, focus on atmosphere to suit the mood, to fill the gap’s of Gil’s very functional review system. The mood ring to Gil’s practical backpack, let’s say, or the friendly barista to help out the backroom cook. Let’s get to it! Drink in peace and for justice, dear friends.


How ratings are decided

Design:

  • 1 - 3 decor and upkeep is sloppy, random; not much thought into overall aesthetic; low maintenance of the space; not a wide variety of seating options; dark; bad music
  • 4 - 7 an attempt was made to decorate, if unfinished; some seating variety, but not enough; some music, perhaps too loud or too quiet; someone tried to decorate but didn’t get quite so far
  • 8 - 10 lovely music to suit the weather, variety of seating options and textural substance (wood, bright, cement, velvet); a coherent sense of style; clear intention behind setting up the space and comfort

People (service & patrons):

  • 1 - 3 the barista is rude and mad at you for some reason; patrons are too loud, take too many selfies, or won’t care to watch your stuff (rare)
  • 4 - 7 the barista doesn’t particularly care about you, but they’re not mad either. you wouldn’t be friends with the patrons but they are fine and nice
  • 8 - 10 Barista remembers your order, and smiles at you; the patrons will share their joint; you might make a new friend!

Coffee:

  • 1 - 3 foam comes bubbly; coffee is weak ; the coffee is so clearly not the point in which case I guess you’re just a cafe that has coffee, too.
  • 4 - 7 Coffee is decent, but maybe the barista is new; maybe the nut and oat milks are off brand; the barista takes the orders, slowing down the process
  • 8 - 10 You can choose your coffee; you trust the barista not to mess it up; latte art; the barista
